Rotoscoping

Rotoscoping is the technique of animating by tracing over live-action video footage frame by frame. Originally patented in 1917, it has evolved into a fine art form used across film, music videos, and digital media. This project documents the full creative process — from raw footage to finished animated output.

Step-by-Step Tutorial

Process

A guided walkthrough of the rotoscoping workflow — from your video reference all the way through to the final exported render.

Step 1

Video Reference

Start with your source footage. Study the motion, lighting, and key poses before beginning any frame work.

Step 2

Import to After Effects

Bring your source video into After Effects. Set up your composition, match the frame rate and resolution to your reference footage, and organize your project panel.

Step 3

Freeze Frame

Freeze the video at key moments to isolate individual frames for tracing. Carefully examine each pose and decide where to place your anchor points and paths.

Step 4

Separate Layers

Break your composition into distinct layers — body parts, background, foreground elements. Keeping layers separate gives you more control when animating and coloring.

Step 5

Trace Each Layer

Go frame by frame and trace over each layer using the pen tool or brushes. Clean outlines here make everything easier downstream.

Step 6

Export the Render

Send your composition to the Render Queue. Configure your output settings and export the final animated sequence as a video file.
