Rotoscoping

The art of tracing over live-action footage frame by frame — originally patented in 1917 and evolved into a fine art form across film, music videos, and digital media. This project documents the full creative process from raw footage to finished animated output.

Process

Step-by-Step Tutorial

A guided walkthrough of the rotoscoping workflow — from source footage all the way through to the final exported render.

Step 01

Video Reference

Start with your source footage. Study the motion, lighting, and key poses before beginning any frame work.

Step 02

Import to After Effects

Bring your source video into After Effects. Set up your composition, match the frame rate and resolution, and organize your project panel.

Step 03

Freeze Frame

Freeze the video at key moments to isolate individual frames for tracing. Examine each pose and decide where to place your anchor points.

Step 04

Separate Layers

Break your composition into distinct layers — body parts, background, foreground. Keeping layers separate gives full control when animating and coloring.

Step 05

Trace Each Layer

Go frame by frame and trace over each layer using the pen tool or brushes. Clean outlines here make everything easier downstream.

Step 06

Export the Render

Send your composition to the Render Queue. Configure output settings and export the final animated sequence as a video file.
