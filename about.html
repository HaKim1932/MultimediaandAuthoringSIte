My Story

The Journey So Far

My path into Information Technology began with a simple but powerful curiosity: how does technology bring ideas to life? That question led me to the Technological University of the Philippines, one of the country's premier science and technology universities, where I enrolled in the Bachelor of Science in Information Technology program.

TUP challenged me not just to learn technology, but to master it — to understand systems from the ground up, to problem-solve under pressure, and to build things that matter. The culture of discipline and excellence shaped how I approach every project I take on.

Now in my second year, third term, I find myself at an exciting intersection — technical knowledge is deepening, creative projects are becoming more ambitious, and the bridge between art and engineering is becoming clearer with every subject I study.

Multimedia & Authoring

How This Subject Shaped Me

Multimedia & Authoring is not a subject you can coast through. It demands both halves of the brain simultaneously — the analytical precision of a programmer and the visual sensitivity of an artist. Here is how it has changed the way I think, create, and present work.

01
🎨
Visual Thinking
Before this subject, I thought primarily in terms of code and logic. Rotoscoping taught me to see differently — to notice how a body moves through space, how weight shifts, how light falls across a face. That visual awareness now informs everything I build.
02
🌌
3D Spatial Reasoning
Modeling the solar system in 3D forced me to understand scale, perspective, and the physics of light in ways no textbook could. I now have an intuitive grasp of three-dimensional space that translates directly into better UI design, data visualization, and systems thinking.
03
⏱
Patience & Precision
Rotoscoping hundreds of frames by hand is a lesson in patience unlike anything else. Each frame demands full attention. That discipline — slowing down, being meticulous, not rushing toward the finish — has made me a more careful and deliberate developer and designer.
04
🔧
Technical Problem Solving
3D rendering is full of unexpected challenges: geometry that doesn't behave, textures that tile incorrectly, lighting that flattens a scene. Working through those problems honed my debugging instincts and made me more comfortable with ambiguity — a crucial skill for any developer.
05
📄
Portfolio Thinking
This subject pushed me to think about how work is presented, not just how it's made. Building this portfolio site from scratch — writing every line of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — taught me that a project is never truly finished until it can be communicated clearly to someone who wasn't there when it was made.
06
🌟
Creative Confidence
Perhaps most importantly, Multimedia & Authoring gave me permission to call myself creative. The intersection of technical skill and artistic vision is exactly where I want to live as a future IT professional — and this subject proved that that intersection is real and reachable.

Looking Forward

Goals & Aspirations

The work done in Multimedia & Authoring is not just coursework — it is a foundation. Here is where I intend to take these skills next.

  • I

    Graduate with Honors from TUP

    My primary academic goal is to complete the BSIT program at the Technological University of the Philippines with the highest academic standing possible — carrying the discipline and work ethic that TUP instills in every student.

  • II

    Pursue UI/UX or Creative Technology

    The intersection of design and engineering that Multimedia & Authoring revealed to me feels like home. I intend to pursue a career that lives in that space — whether as a UI/UX designer, a creative technologist, or a front-end developer with a strong visual sensibility.

  • III

    Deepen 3D and Motion Skills

    The solar system rendering project opened a door. I want to continue developing in Blender — exploring character modeling, motion graphics, architectural visualization, and eventually real-time rendering in game engines like Unity or Unreal.

    Build a Professional Portfolio

    This academic portfolio is just the beginning. By graduation, I intend to have a body of work that demonstrates both technical competence and creative range — projects that solve real problems and tell compelling visual stories at the same time.

    The Technological University of the Philippines gave me opportunity. One day I want to return that — as a mentor, an industry partner, or simply a proud alumna whose work reflects well on the institution and the people who made it possible.
