Multimedia & Authoring

How This Subject Shaped Me

Multimedia & Authoring demands both halves of the brain simultaneously — the analytical precision of a programmer and the visual sensitivity of an artist. Here is how it changed the way I think, create, and present work.

01
🎨
Visual Thinking
Before this subject, I thought primarily in code and logic. Rotoscoping taught me to see differently — to notice how a body moves through space, how weight shifts, how light falls. That visual awareness now informs everything I build.
02
🌍
3D Spatial Reasoning
Modeling the solar system in 3D forced me to grasp scale, perspective, and light physics in ways no textbook could. I now have an intuitive command of three-dimensional space that carries into UI design, data visualization, and systems thinking.
03
⏱
Patience & Precision
Rotoscoping hundreds of frames by hand is a lesson in patience unlike anything else. Each frame demands full attention. That discipline — slowing down, being meticulous, refusing to rush — made me a more careful and deliberate developer and designer.
04
🔧
Technical Problem Solving
3D rendering is full of surprises: geometry that misbehaves, textures that tile wrong, lighting that flattens a scene. Working through those challenges honed my debugging instincts and made me far more comfortable with ambiguity.
05
📄
Portfolio Thinking
This subject pushed me to think about how work is presented, not just made. Building this portfolio site from scratch — writing every line of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — proved that a project isn't finished until it can be communicated clearly.
06
✨
Creative Confidence
Perhaps most importantly, Multimedia & Authoring gave me permission to call myself creative. The intersection of technical skill and artistic vision is exactly where I want to live as a future IT professional — and this subject proved it's real and reachable.

    My primary goal is to complete the BSIT program at the Technological University of the Philippines — finishing every term with dedication, carrying TUP's discipline and work ethic all the way through to graduation day.

    The intersection of design and engineering that Multimedia & Authoring revealed feels like home. I intend to pursue a career that lives in that space — whether as a UI/UX designer, a creative technologist, or a front-end developer with a strong visual sensibility.
